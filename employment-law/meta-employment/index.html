What makes a case

Being fired isn't illegal. Being fired for these reasons is.

California protects employees from being let go for the wrong reasons. If any of these describe your situation, you may have a claim.

01

Discrimination

Terminated because of age, race, gender, religion, disability, pregnancy, sexual orientation, or national origin.

02

Retaliation

Fired after reporting harassment, discrimination, safety issues, or illegal conduct.

03

Wrongful Termination

Fired for an unlawful reason.

04

Medical & Disability Leave

Punished or replaced after requesting leave, an accommodation, or time to recover.

05

Unpaid Wages & Overtime

Denied final pay, overtime, commissions, or legally required meal and rest breaks.

06

Harassment & Sexual Harassment

Subjected to a hostile work environment or sexually harassed.

Questions people ask after being fired.

California is an at-will state, so employers can end employment for many reasons — but not illegal ones. If you were let go because of a protected characteristic, for reporting misconduct, for taking protected leave, or in violation of a contract, you may have a claim. The fastest way to find out is the free case check above.

Deadlines vary by the type of claim, and some are shorter than people expect. Because evidence and witnesses fade over time, the sooner you speak with an attorney after being let go, the better. If your situation is older, it's still worth a quick call to check.
